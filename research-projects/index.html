Research Projects

Reduced Order Modelling and Scientific Machine Learning (SciML)

Model order reduction provides a low-dimensional representation of high-fidelity simulations while preserving essential flow and structural dynamics. Depending on the chosen approximation and projection strategy, several ROM frameworks exist — including intrusive, non-intrusive, and hybrid data-driven approaches. Recent developments in physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) enable coupling governing equations with sparse datasets in a non-intrusive setting.

My ongoing work involves three aspects: (1) Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) that embed discretized reduced-order equations as loss terms (ANN-DisPINN, LSTM-DisPINN). (2) Adaptive nonlinear filter–regularized projection-based ROMs for quasi-geostrophic equations relevant to oceanographic flow. (3) Data-driven ROMs (ANN-GPOD) capable of integrating both numerical and experimental datasets for industrial flow configurations.

Linear and Non-Linear Non-Intrusive ROM for Aeroelastic and Wave–Structure Interaction Problems

During my Ph.D. at the National University of Singapore under Prof. Khoo Boo Cheong, I developed several linear and nonlinear non-intrusive reduced-order models for aeroelastic systems and gust load analysis. The open-source CFD platform SU2 was extended to perform coupled fluid–structure simulations and modal ROM computations.

Signal Interpolation Augmented Linear Non-Intrusive ROM — A linear ROM based on subspace identification combined with the Discrete Empirical Interpolation Method (DEIM) for reconstructing distributed variables. Applied to airfoil flutter under gust excitation; the inviscid flutter boundary for NACA64A010 is shown below.

Nonlinear Non-Intrusive ROM — A deep learning–enhanced ROM coupling Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks with DEIM for modeling viscous transonic aeroelastic phenomena.

Periodic Gust Effect on Aeroelastic Motion — Assessment of aeroelastic responses due to periodic vertical gusts impacting a pitching–plunging airfoil with trailing-edge control surfaces in transonic regimes.

Three-Dimensional Aeroelasticity and ROM in SU2 — Implementation of modal analysis for linear aeroelastic ROM computation in SU2. The code enables modal deflections and random excitations for 3D wings, extending SU2 beyond rigid body motion.

Wave–Structure Interaction — Evaluation of LSTM-driven Non-Intrusive ROMs (NIROM) for floating-body dynamics under surface waves. Hydrodynamic forces and displacements are derived via SPH simulations to benchmark ROM performance.

Miscellaneous

Additional research includes fluid–structure interactions in microfluidic systems for bioengineering applications. In particular, droplet generation in flexible microchannels mimicking cellular motion through capillary networks.

Droplet Dynamics in Flexible Microchannels — Experimental and analytical studies on pressure-actuated membrane walls for tunable droplet generation and monodisperse control at high capillary numbers.
